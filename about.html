About

USER RESEARCH

As a user researcher, I am passionate about understanding user needs, motivations, and behaviors through research and translating human insights into improved user and customer experiences. I have worked as a user researcher in two start-up companies where I lead and conduct all phases of user research – study design, protocol development, participant recruitment, prototyping, data collection/analysis, and report/presentation. Throughout my previous roles, I have exercised many methodologies from heuristic evaluations, to qualitative interviewing, to survey design. I embed user-centered thinking throughout the product development process and collaborate with cross-functional stakeholders to drive strategic design impacts through research.

SOCIAL SCIENCE BACKGROUND

My research focuses on the psychological processing of media messages; attitude and behavior change; emergent technologies and society; and advanced research methods. My research has been published in ANNALS of the International Communication Association, Mass Communication & Society, and Journal of Broadcasting & Electronic Media.

My dissertation, titled “Group Identity versus Relational Identity: The Influence of Identity Primes on Socio-political Attitudes,” explores the different effects of identities on people’s interpretations of communication about politically polarized issues in the United States. This dissertation offers strategic communication strategies that can be used to influence people’s interpretations of messages about socio-political issues and ultimately impact their attitude and behaviors.

I currently teach in Klein College of Media and Communication at Temple University. I have taught undergraduate courses on Research Methods, Media Criticism, Public Information Campaign, Global Media, among others.

I completed my Ph.D. at the Klein College of Media and Communication at Temple University in 2020. I hold an M.A. in Communication from Hong Kong Baptist University and B.A. in Advertising from Hunan University of Technology and Business.

MEDIA INDUSTRY

Before pursuing my doctoral degree, I had worked in a variety of media outlets as an assistant editor, a journalist, and a reporter. My work experience in the media industry developed my communication, collaboration, storytelling, and problem-solving skills. It also improved my ability to work under pressure, meet deadlines, and make decisions quickly. My most rewarding experience during those years came from interviewing people from diverse social classes and occupations. It taught me to become an empathetic listener.
